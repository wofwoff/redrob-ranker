# Ideal-candidate archetypes for the Senior AI Engineer (retrieval/ranking) role.
# Blank-line separated. Written in the plain first-person voice the dataset uses,
# so TF-IDF similarity rewards prose-buried fit, not buzzword density.

Senior applied ML engineer with six years of experience, four of them building
and shipping embeddings-based retrieval and ranking systems to real users at a
product company. I built a hybrid dense plus BM25 retrieval pipeline serving
recruiter search, owned the vector index refresh and embedding drift, and drove
offline NDCG and MRR evaluation tied to online A/B tests.

I built a recommendation and search system at a consumer-internet product
company. My work covered candidate retrieval with FAISS and a learning-to-rank
re-ranker, relevance evaluation with NDCG and MAP, and shipping the model to
production with latency and throughput constraints. Strong Python, comfortable
owning the whole pipeline from data to serving.

Applied scientist who shipped a semantic search feature backed by sentence
transformer embeddings and a Pinecone vector database. I designed the offline
evaluation harness, correlated it with online engagement, fine-tuned encoders
with LoRA, and iterated fast on a scrappy first version before optimizing.

Machine learning engineer focused on information retrieval and ranking at scale.
Deployed dense retrieval and hybrid search, tuned re-ranking models, and built
the recommendation infrastructure that decides what users see. I care about
retrieval quality regression, index freshness, and rigorous ranking evaluation.

Product-minded ML engineer: I would rather ship a working ranker in a week and
learn from real recruiter behavior than perfect an offline metric. Built vector
search and matching systems, ran experiments, and owned the retrieval and
ranking layer of the product end to end.
